Unsaturated Soil Analysis in Garden Grove

Together, we solve the challenges of tomorrow.

DETAILS →

Garden Grove sits on the coastal plain of Orange County, where the water table fluctuates seasonally and the upper soils dry out significantly during summer months. This cyclic wetting and drying creates partially saturated conditions that standard saturated-soil mechanics cannot predict. Our unsaturated soil analysis directly measures matric suction and collapse potential, giving you realistic parameters for foundation design on the expansive clays common in this area. Before we run this analysis we often recommend a georradar survey to map shallow anomalies that could affect suction profiles.

In Garden Grove's expansive soils, ignoring unsaturated behavior can underestimate foundation movement by a factor of three during dry cycles.

Approach and scope

We follow ASTM D5298 for filter paper suction measurement and ASTM D6836 for soil-water characteristic curves. For Garden Grove projects, the SWCC is particularly relevant because the native Alo silt loam and San Emigdio soils exhibit high shrink-swell potential when moisture changes. Our laboratory procedure includes:
  • Matric suction measurement via dew-point hygrometer (WP4C) for rapid results
  • Volumetric shrinkage and collapse index per ASTM D5333
  • Unsaturated shear strength testing using modified triaxial cells with controlled suction
These parameters feed directly into bearing capacity and heave calculations for slabs-on-grade and shallow foundations.

Site-specific factors

Garden Grove has over 170,000 residents and lies within the Santa Ana River floodplain, where fine-grained soils dominate the upper 6 meters. During the 2014 La Habra earthquake sequence, several residential slabs in the city experienced differential heave attributed to suction changes in unsaturated clays. Without proper unsaturated soil analysis, a foundation designed for saturated conditions may settle or heave 2–5 cm more than predicted. That movement cracks drywall, tilts garage slabs, and compromises structural integrity. We quantify these risks before construction so you can mitigate them with proper moisture management.

Related technical services

01

Soil-Water Characteristic Curve (SWCC) Testing

Using pressure plate extractors and dew-point hygrometers, we generate the SWCC for your site's soil. This curve tells us how suction changes with water content, which is essential for modeling heave and collapse in Garden Grove's expansive clays.

02

Unsaturated Shear Strength Testing

We perform modified triaxial and direct shear tests under controlled matric suction. The results provide effective stress parameters that account for partial saturation, giving you realistic safety factors for retaining walls and shallow foundations.

03

Shrink-Swell and Collapse Potential Analysis

Our lab measures volumetric shrinkage, swell pressure, and collapse index per ASTM D5333. For Garden Grove projects, we correlate these values with local soil survey data to predict long-term movement under seasonal moisture changes.

Relevant standards

ASTM D5298 – Standard Test Method for Measurement of Soil Potential (Suction) Using Filter Paper, ASTM D6836 – Standard Test Methods for Determination of the Soil Water Characteristic Curve, ASTM D5333 – Standard Test Method for Measurement of Collapse Potential of Soils, IBC 2021 – Chapter 18: Soils and Foundations (expansive soil provisions)
